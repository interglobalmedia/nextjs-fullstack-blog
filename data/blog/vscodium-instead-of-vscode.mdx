Every time there is a `major update` to `VSCode`, some Microsoft related
`proprietary issue` seems to come up.

The other day, I went into `VSCode`, and saw that the `user interface` and
`user experience` had `radically changed`, including certain `Github related`
settings. Now, `VScode` has `integrated support` for managing `GitHub` issues
`directly within` the `editor`, allowing users to `create`, `view`, and `work`
on `issues` without `leaving` the `environment`. This `integration` is
`facilitated` through the `GitHub Pull Requests and Issues` extension, which
`enhances productivity` by `linking issues` to the `code context`. In
fact,` now` you are `required` to `install` and `activate` the
`GitHub Pull Requests and Issues` extension in `VSCode`. If you still want to
use `VSCode` despite this `requirement`, you can visit
[Working with GitHub in VS Code](https://code.visualstudio.com/docs/sourcecontrol/github)
in the `VSCode` documentation.

## So what is VSCodiium and why use it instead of VSCode

- First and foremost, you `don't have to use` the
  `VSCode GitHub Pull Requests and Issues` extension in `VSCodium`, and you
  don't have to have `proprietary AI integrated` into your `text editor`.
  Honestly, I did not recognize `VSCode` at all.
- Second of all, the `VSCode license` is `proprietary`, whereas the
  `VSCodium license` is `open source` (MIT).
    - Because `VSCodium` is `open source`, it `allows users` to `modify` and
      `redistribute` the `software`.
    - `VSCode`'s `source code` IS `available`, BUT the `downloaded version` is
      `proprietary` and therefore `includes restrictions`.
- `VSCode` telemetry, which is the collection of usage data and error reports
  from `VSCode` to help improve the software, is `enabled` by `default`.
  Therefore, `VSCode` tracks user behavior and sends data back to `Microsoft`,
  which some users may find intrusive.
    - `VSCodium` telemetry is `not enabled` by `default`. Therefore, `VSCodium`
      `does not collect` any `telemetry` data, ensuring `greater privacy` for
      `users`.
- `VSCode` does `support` a `wider range` of `extensions`, including
  `proprietary` ones.
    - `VSCodium` focuses on `open-source` extensions, so `users` may `need` to
      `manually install` some `extensions`.
- `VSCode` benefits from `extensive support` and `documentation` because it is
  `owned` by `Microsoft`.
    - Because `VSCodium` is `community-driven` and `open source`, it has
      `less formal support` but is `maintained` by `open-source developers`.
        - `VSCodium` is usually considered `better` for `users` who prioritize
          `privacy`, `open-source principles`, and the `ability` to modify
          `software` without `restrictions`.

`Once` I `re-added` my `rsa_pub` key on `Github`, it was as usual `interacting`
with `Github` via `VSCodium`. My public `SSH key` had `expired` and was
`no longer valid` on `Github` because I `had not interacted` with `Github` for
more than a year.
