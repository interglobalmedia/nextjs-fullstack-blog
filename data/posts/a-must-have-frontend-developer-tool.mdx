***Today*** I was ***adding*** a ***new project*** `image` to ***my*** `front end developer portfolio`, and I ***had*** the ***damnedest*** time ***trying*** to ***match*** its `size` to ***all*** the ***others***, which I ***had created*** with the
[FireShot Screen Capture](https://chrome.google.com/webstore/detail/take-webpage-screenshots/mcbpblocgmgfnpjjppndjkmgjaogfceg) `Chrome` ***extension*** at ***another time***. The ***trick*** to ***making all*** your `screen captures`
the ***same size*** is in the `size` of the `browser window`. But ***how*** was I ***going*** to figure ***that out***? I was ***getting*** `sick` and `tired` of ***redoing*** all `screen captures` ***just because*** they ***weren’t*** all ***taken*** at the ***same time***, ***with*** the ***probability*** that
the `images` ***would*** end up ***being*** `different sizes`. An ***experienced*** `Frontend Developer` ***knows*** that ***all*** `images` ***within*** a ***given*** `element` ***should be*** the ***same size*** in ***order*** for the `layout` to ***work***.

I ***took*** it ***upon myself*** to ***find*** a `tool` that would ***provide me*** with `browser window size`. I ***came up*** with ***just*** the ***right one*** in my `Google`` ***search***:

- [How Big Is My Browser?](http://howbigismybrowser.com/)

It ***does*** exactly ***what*** it ***says***. It ***tells*** you `how big` your `browser window` ***is***. What’s ***even better***, is that you can ***resize*** it if the `size` ***changes*** on you! ***No more*** `guessing games`. ***No more*** `re-dos`. ***No more*** `time wasting`. ***Check*** it ***out***!

*Happy* `Frontend` *developing*!
