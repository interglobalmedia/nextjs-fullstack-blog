Like so many others, the medical center I am a patient at and where I go to see my doctors uses `mychart`.

I have never had a problem adding a doctor's appointment to my iPhone's calendar app. Adding an appointment to the iPhone calendar app is just a click away!

However, adding a doctor's appointment to macOS desktop/laptop is not as intuitive.

When I go into mychart and click on "Add to calendar" inside an. appointment, it immediately adds the appointment to my iPhone's calendar app. I also make sure to click on "Include Details" so that all the information I need related to the appointment is also included in the appointment detaiuls in the Calendar app.

I only found out the other day how I can quickly add a doctor's appointment to the Calendar app on my Macbook Pro.

First, as before, I have to click on "Add to Calendar" inside the appointment I want to add located on mychart.

When I click on "Add to Calendar", it downloads to the Downloads folder as a `.ics` file. And in order to import this appointment to the Calendar app, all I have to do is double click on it. This opens the Calendar app, and the appointment is automatically imported into the correct date and time.

before I knew how to correctly import appointments, I did the following:

- First, I downloaded the appointment .ics file onto my Macbook Pro. It saved to my ~/Downloards directoryb as a .ics file.
- Second, I dragged the .ics file onto my desktop.
- Third, I dragged the .ics file into my Calendar app within the correct date of the appointment.
- Fourth, I had to change the time zone. It always defaulted to GMT, which meant that I had to manually change it to EST, ET, or EDT.

After a while, this became really annoying! So I did a Google AI Search and came up with the above solution.
