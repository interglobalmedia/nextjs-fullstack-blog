Like so many others, the medical center I am a patient at and where I go to see my doctors uses `mychart`.

## Importing appointments from mychart into the iPhone Calendar app

I have never had a problem adding a doctor's appointment to my iPhone's `Calendar app`. Adding an appointment to the iPhone `Calendar app` is just a click away!

When I go into `mychart` and click on "Add to Calendar" inside an appointment, it immediately adds the appointment to my iPhone's `Calendar app`. I also make sure to click on "Include Details" so that all the information I need related to the appointment is also included in the `Calendar app`.

## Importing appointments from mychart into the macOS Calendar app the wrong way

However, adding an appointment to the macOS desktop/laptop is not as intuitive.

Before I knew how to correctly import appointments, I did the following:

- First, I downloaded the appointment `.ics` file onto my Macbook Pro. It saved to my `~/Downloards` directory as a `.ics` file.
- Second, I dragged the `.ics` file onto my desktop.
- Third, I dragged the `.ics` file into my Calendar app within the correct date of the appointment.
- Fourth, I had to change the time zone. It always defaulted to `GMT`, which meant that I had to manually change it to `ET`.

After a while, this became really annoying! So I did a `Google AI Search` and came up with the below solution.

## Importing appointments from mychart into the macOS Calendar app the right way

I only found out the other day how I can quickly add a doctor's appointment to the `Calendar app` on my Macbook Pro.

First, as before, I have to click on "Add to Calendar" inside the appointment I want to add. And when prompted, I make sure to click on "Include Details" as well.

When I click on "Add to Calendar", it downloads to the `Downloads` directory as a `.ics` file. And in order to import this appointment to the `Calendar app`, all I have to do is double click on it. This opens the `Calendar app`, and the appointment is automatically imported into the correct date and time.
